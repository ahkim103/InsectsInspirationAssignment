Locusts....Cuisine?!

Indeed. Locusts are eaten by some people!



Where Are They Eaten?

Eating locusts was a widespread practice in areas where swarms occured, including Africa, the Middle East, Asia, and even North America!

Health Benefits?

Locusts are a good source of protein, but is missing methionine, an essential amino acid. Locusts also contain adequate amounts of iodine, phosphorus, iron, thiamine, riboflavin, niacin, as well as traces of calcium, magnesium and selenium.

What Do They Taste Like?

Some people describe cooked locust as similar to smoky flavored bacon and reasonably tasty. But be careful when trying locusts in foregin countries since sanitary praticies might be inadequate compared to the US.

Interesting Locust Dishes?

If you're interested in locust dishes and how they may be prepared and served, here's two dishes you might find interesting: Chocolate Covered Locusts & Locust Kebabs!




But Hold Your Horses!

Given that governments are now turning to chemicals and pesticides to manage the outbreaks and swarms, the correct advice is that people should not eat the locusts or use them for animal feed since these chemicals pose a major health risk.
