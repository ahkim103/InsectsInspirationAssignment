Locusts In General Media

How Are Locusts Usually Depicted In General Media?

Locusts have typically represented greed and destruction in books, movies, and even religion.



Personal Book Example: Bone


The Bone Series (Jeff Smith) was one of my favorite comic book series that I have read when I was young due to its interesting story and unique characters. In this series, the main overarching antagonist is referred to as The Lord of the Locusts, or simply The Locust. The Locust is a nightmarish spirit without any shape or form that wanted to become part of the physical world. To do this, he needed to possess the body of a mortal, which he eventually decided on Mim, the queen of the dragons who kept all life in perfect balance. Once The Locust possessed Mim, she went mad, causing the balance to crumble.

To save the world, the dragons reluctantly rebelled and battled against Mim, claiming many of their lives. With no other option, the dragons were forced to turn her into stone and entomb her beneath the earth, trapping the Locust in her. The land in which the battle had took place, would come to be known as the Valley, where the main story takes place. Although The Locust himself is not present until much later in the series, he influences a lot of what happens throughout the books, including creating a swarm of locusts that seperate the three main characters early in the first book.



Movie Example: Jurassic World Dominion


In this movie clip, a swarm of giant locusts descend and attack a local farm, destroying most of the crops, while leaving certain crops intact? As it turns out, these locusts were genetically modified and created by Biosyn Genetics and are based on the desert locust and the extinct species of large Maastrichtian-era locust, which increases their size and strength but also makes them unable to devour crops engineered by Biosyn Genetics.

As it turns out, these locusts were used for illegal biological warfare against rival Biosyn companies and independent farmers. This scheme is eventually exposed and stopped later in the movie. This clip and movie emphasizes not only the danger of locusts but also the dangers of biological warfare involving insects and bioweapons.
